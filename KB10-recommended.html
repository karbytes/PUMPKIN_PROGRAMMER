Websites

Below are some excellent online resources for hungry minds.

Evolution and Genetics

  • 23andMe
    A fun way to learn about your genetic heritage.

  • Darwin Online
    A complete collection of Charles Darwin’s writings.

  • Human Genome Project
    One of the most revolutionary feats of the 21st century.

History of Computing

  • Internet Archive
    A time­-capsule of the Information Age.

  • Computer History Museum
    A great Silicon Valley destination and treasure trove.

  • Intel Museum
    A tour of the microchip’s invention and much Moore!

Online Education

  • Code School
    A gamified approach to learning web programming.

  • Khan Academy
    A free online resource for video tutoring on multiple subjects.

  • Coursera
    Free online courses on a variety of subjects.

Philosophy and Logic

  • Humanity+
    Learn about the exciting transhumanist movement.

  • Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy
    A comprehensive collection of articles on philosophy.

  • Thou Shalt Not Commit Logical Fallacies
    A great list of logical blunders to avoid when making an argument.

Science and Tech News

  • Popular Science
    A magazine about the latest happenings in science.

  • Singularity Hub
    A futuristic science and technology magazine.

  • Wired Magazine
    A magazine about trends in technology and gadgets.

Books

Below are some great books for brawny brains.

Biology and Evolution

  • The Blind Watchmaker​ by Richard Dawkins (1986)
    A compelling case for evolution and how it works.

  • Brain Bugs b​y Dean Buonomano (2011)
    A book about the ways our brains are prone to mistakes.

  • Darwin’s Dangerous Idea ​by Dan Dennett (1995)
    An illuminating book about how algorithms govern the natural world.

Computer Science and Programming

  • The Art of Programming​ by Donald Knuth (1968)
    A classic that belongs in every programmer’s library.

  • Cracking the Coding Interview ​by Gayle McDowell (2011)
    A helpful resource for aspiring software engineers.

  • Hacker’s Delight ​by Henry Warren (2012)
    A recipe book of algorithms and programming problems.

The Future

  • Abundance​ by Peter Diamandis and Steven Kotler (2012)
    An optimistic and rational look at where humanity is going.

  • The Age of Spiritual Machines b​y Ray Kurzweil (2008)
    “Prophecies” about how machines will surpass human ability.

  • Evolving Ourselves ​by Juan Enriquez and Steve Gullans (2015)
    A book about how evolution and human beings interact.

Philosophy of Mind

  • Consciousness Explained ​by Daniel Dennett (1991)
    A compelling theory of how the “soul” the software of the brain.

  • Freedom Evolves ​by Daniel Dennett (2003)
    An argument for how (limited) freedom can exist in a deterministic universe.

  • I Am A Strange Loop​ by Douglas Hofstadter (2007)
    A fascinating description of consciousness as a self-­referencing “loop.”

Science and the Cosmos

  • Billions and Billions ​by Carl Sagan (1998)
    An inspiring last book by an influential astronomer.

  • QED​ by Richard Feynman (1988)
    A layperson’s introduction to quantum electrodynamics.

  • A Universe from Nothing ​by Lawrence Krauss (2013)
    An interesting speculation on the universe’s past and future.
